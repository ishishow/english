## 20.1 重要エラー：deserve / reserve の混同

```
❌ Bad: "I deserved the half marathon"
✅ Good: "I reserved a spot for the half marathon" / "I signed up for the half marathon"
```

### deserve vs reserve

| 単語 | 意味 | 例文 |
|------|------|------|
| deserve | 〜に値する | You deserve a break. |
| reserve | 予約する | I reserved a table. |

```
❌ Bad: "I deserve a ticket"（意味が違う）
✅ Good: "I reserved a ticket" / "I booked a ticket"
```

## 20.2 重要エラー：形容詞と副詞の混同

```
❌ Bad: "Don't be seriously about that"
✅ Good: "Don't be serious about that"
      または "Don't take it seriously"
```

### パターン

| 構文 | 正しい形 | 例文 |
|------|---------|------|
| be + 形容詞 | serious | Don't be serious. |
| 動詞 + 副詞 | seriously | Don't take it seriously. |

## 20.8 日本文化：除夜の鐘

### 除夜の鐘を説明する

```
In Japan, we ring the temple bell 108 times on New Year's Eve.
日本では大晦日にお寺の鐘を108回鳴らします。

This tradition is called "Joya no Kane."
この伝統は「除夜の鐘」と呼ばれます。

The 108 rings represent the 108 worldly desires, called "bonno" in Japanese.
108回は、日本語で「煩悩」と呼ばれる108の世俗的欲望を表しています。

By ringing the bell, we purify ourselves of these desires.
鐘を鳴らすことで、これらの欲望から自分を清めます。
```

### 数字108の由来

```
The number 108 comes from:
- 6 senses (sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch, mind)
- 3 types of feelings (pleasant, unpleasant, neutral)
- 2 states of awareness (aware, not aware)
- 3 dimensions of time (past, present, future)
6 × 3 × 2 × 3 = 108
```

## 20.12 練習問題 解答

### A. deserve と reserve
1. You **deserve** a reward for your hard work.
2. I **reserved** a table at the restaurant.
3. He **deserves** to win the prize.
4. Please **reserve** a seat for me.

### B. 正しい形
1. Don't be **serious** about that.
2. I don't like **running**.
3. Growth is **highly related** / **closely connected** to challenges.
4. When I was **a child**, I played outside.

### C. 日本語を英語に
1. Getting out of your comfort zone is important.
2. Not giving up is an effective approach.
3. Failure is part of the process.
